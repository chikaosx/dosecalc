Learn Drug Calculations

1. Basic Dose Calculation

Persona: Aisha, a 2nd-year nursing student on placement in A&E.
Scenario: Aisha is asked to administer 250mg of paracetamol. Available stock is 500mg tablets.
Formula: (Want / Have) × Volume = (250 / 500) × 1 = 0.5 tablets
Explanation: You want 250mg, and you have 500mg tablets. Divide what you want by what you have and multiply by the volume per tablet (usually 1 tablet). Administer 0.5 tablets.

2. IV Infusion Rate

Persona: Jack, a newly qualified nurse in ICU.
Scenario: A 500mL saline bag must run over 5 hours.
Formula: Volume ÷ Time = 500 ÷ 5 = 100 mL/hr
Explanation: Divide total volume by number of hours to get rate per hour.

3. Drops per Minute

Persona: Leah, a paediatric nurse using a manual IV drip.
Scenario: Give 100mL in 30 minutes with a 20 drops/mL set.
Formula: (Volume × Drop Factor) ÷ Time = (100 × 20) ÷ 30 = 67 drops/min
Explanation: Multiply volume by drop factor and divide by time in minutes.

4. Weight-Based Dose

Persona: Joe, a pharmacist checking doses for a child.
Scenario: Child weighs 20kg, drug prescribed at 10mg/kg.
Formula: Weight × Dose/kg = 20 × 10 = 200 mg
Explanation: Multiply weight by prescribed dose per kilogram.

5. Clark’s Rule

Persona: Diana, a community nurse administering a paediatric dose.
Scenario: Adult dose is 300mg. Child weighs 50 lbs.
Formula: (Weight in lbs / 150) × Adult dose = (50 / 150) × 300 = 100 mg
Explanation: A formula for approximating children’s doses from adult doses.

6. BSA-Based Dose

Persona: Dr. Patel, a paediatric oncologist.
Scenario: BSA = 0.9 m², Adult dose = 400mg.
Formula: (BSA / 1.73) × Adult dose = (0.9 / 1.73) × 400 = 208 mg
Explanation: Used especially for chemo and paediatric meds. Normal adult BSA = 1.73 m².

7. Percent Solution

Persona: Emily, preparing a lidocaine solution.
Scenario: 2% solution, 10mL needed.
Formula: % × 10 × Volume = 2 × 10 × 10 = 200 mg
Explanation: 1% = 10mg/mL. Multiply concentration by volume.

8. Insulin Dose

Persona: Paul, a diabetic patient.
Scenario: Meal has 90g carbs, ratio is 1 unit per 15g carbs.
Formula: Carbs / Ratio = 90 / 15 = 6 units
Explanation: Divide total carbs by your carb ratio.

9. Reconstitution Dose

Persona: Sarah, reconstituting antibiotics.
Scenario: Prescribed 250mg, stock is 125mg/mL.
Formula: Dose / Concentration = 250 / 125 = 2 mL
Explanation: Divide dose needed by concentration per mL.

10. Heparin Infusion

Persona: Mohammed, cardiac nurse in CCU.
Scenario: 70kg patient, 18 units/kg/hr, 100 units/mL.
Formula: (70 × 18) / 100 = 12.6 mL/hr
Explanation: Multiply weight by dose per kg, divide by drug concentration.

11. Titration Dosing

Persona: Helen, ICU nurse titrating sedatives.
Scenario: Start at 2mL/hr, increase by 1mL/hr every 15 min until 6mL/hr.
Steps:
- 0 min: 2 mL/hr
- 15 min: 3 mL/hr
- 30 min: 4 mL/hr
- 45 min: 5 mL/hr
- 60 min: 6 mL/hr
Explanation: Titration increases the dose in steps at time intervals.
